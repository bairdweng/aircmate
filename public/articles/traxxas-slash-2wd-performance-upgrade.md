## Introduction: Building a Competitive 2WD Short Course Truck

The Traxxas Slash 2WD is one of the most popular entry-level RC vehicles, but with the right upgrades, it can become a serious competitor in short course racing. This guide covers the essential performance upgrades that will transform your Slash 2WD into a track-ready machine.

## Chapter 1: Power System Upgrades

### 1.1 Brushless Motor System
**Recommended Product: Castle Creations Sidewinder SCT 3800KV**

Upgrading from the stock brushed motor to a brushless system is the single most impactful performance improvement you can make.

**Installation Steps:**
1. Remove stock motor mounting screws
2. Install new brushless motor with proper alignment
3. Connect to ESC with correct polarity
4. Adjust motor pinion to spur gear mesh

**Technical Specifications:**
- **KV Rating:** 3800KV (ideal for 2WD applications)
- **Power Output:** 2S-3S LiPo compatible
- **Torque:** Significantly higher than stock
- **Efficiency:** 85%+ efficiency rating

**Performance Benefits:**
- 40-60% increase in top speed
- Better throttle response and acceleration
- Reduced battery consumption
- Longer runtime per charge

### 1.2 ESC Upgrade
**Recommended Product: Hobbywing Xerun XR8 SCT**

A high-quality ESC provides precise control and advanced programming options essential for competitive racing.

**Key Features:**
- **Programmable Throttle Curves:** Customize acceleration response
- **Drag Brake Settings:** Adjustable from 0-100%
- **Temperature Protection:** Automatic thermal shutdown
- **Low Voltage Cutoff:** Protects LiPo batteries
- **Data Logging:** Monitor performance metrics

**Programming Options:**
- Punch Control: Adjust initial acceleration
- Timing Advance: Optimize for different track conditions
- Brake Force: Fine-tune stopping power
- Reverse Delay: Safety feature for racing

## Chapter 2: Suspension and Chassis Upgrades

### 2.1 Aluminum A-Arms and Suspension Components
**Recommended Product: GPM Racing Aluminum Upgrade Set**

Replace plastic suspension components with aluminum for improved durability and handling consistency.

**Components Included:**
- Front and rear A-arms
- Caster blocks
- Rear hub carriers
- Steering knuckles

**Installation Tips:**
- Use thread locker on all metal-to-metal connections
- Check for proper suspension geometry after installation
- Ensure free movement without binding
- Adjust camber and toe settings for optimal handling

**Performance Impact:**
- Eliminates flex in suspension components
- More predictable handling characteristics
- Increased durability for aggressive driving
- Better cornering stability

### 2.2 Oil-Filled Shocks
**Recommended Product: Traxxas Big Bore Shock Set**

Upgrade from stock friction shocks to oil-filled shocks for consistent damping performance.

**Setup Recommendations:**
- **Front Shocks:** 30-35wt oil, medium springs
- **Rear Shocks:** 25-30wt oil, soft-medium springs
- **Ride Height:** 25-30mm from chassis to ground
- **Sag:** 30-40% of total travel

**Tuning Options:**
- Different weight shock oils for various track conditions
- Spring rate selection based on driving style
- Piston hole configurations for damping characteristics

## Chapter 3: Drivetrain and Transmission

### 3.1 Metal Transmission Gears
**Recommended Product: Hot Racing Metal Transmission Gears**

Upgrade plastic transmission gears to metal for increased durability under high-power brushless systems.

**Gear Set Includes:**
- Input gear
- Idler gear
- Output gear
- Differential gears

**Installation Procedure:**
1. Remove transmission case
2. Replace plastic gears with metal counterparts
3. Use high-quality grease for lubrication
4. Ensure proper gear mesh and alignment

**Benefits:**
- Handles brushless power without stripping
- Reduced maintenance requirements
- Consistent performance under load
- Longer service life

### 3.2 Upgraded Differential
**Recommended Product: MIP Ball Differential**

Replace the stock gear differential with a ball differential for better traction control and tuning options.

**Setup Guidelines:**
- **Tightness:** Adjust based on track conditions
- **Lubrication:** Use specific diff grease
- **Maintenance:** Regular cleaning and adjustment

**Tuning Advantages:**
- Adjustable lock-up characteristics
- Better power distribution to rear wheels
- Reduced tire wear
- Improved corner exit acceleration

## Performance Testing and Setup

### Baseline Testing
Before making upgrades, establish performance baselines:
- **0-30mph Acceleration:** Measure with GPS or timing system
- **Top Speed:** Record maximum velocity
- **Handling:** Lap times on standard course
- **Durability:** Track component wear rates

### Post-Upgrade Evaluation
After each upgrade, retest to measure improvements:
- Compare acceleration and top speed
- Evaluate handling characteristics
- Monitor battery consumption
- Check for any new issues or imbalances

## Maintenance Schedule

**Daily Maintenance:**
- Clean chassis and components
- Check for loose screws and connections
- Inspect tires and wheels
- Verify electronics operation

**Weekly Maintenance:**
- Clean and lubricate transmission
- Check shock oil levels
- Inspect suspension components
- Test radio system functionality

**Monthly Maintenance:**
- Complete teardown and inspection
- Replace worn components
- Rebuild shocks and differential
- Calibrate electronics

## Competition Setup Recommendations

### High-Traction Track Setup
- **Tires:** Pro-Line Hole Shot M3
- **Shock Oil:** 35wt front, 30wt rear
- **Ride Height:** 25mm
- **Camber:** -1° front, -2° rear
- **Toe:** 1° out front, 2° in rear

### Low-Traction Track Setup
- **Tires:** Pro-Line Blockade
- **Shock Oil:** 30wt front, 25wt rear
- **Ride Height:** 30mm
- **Camber:** -0.5° front, -1.5° rear
- **Toe:** 2° out front, 3° in rear

## Conclusion: Building Your Ultimate Slash 2WD

By systematically upgrading your Traxxas Slash 2WD, you can transform it from a basic RTR vehicle into a competitive racing machine. The key is to approach upgrades methodically, testing each modification to understand its impact on performance.

**Recommended Upgrade Priority:**
1. Brushless power system
2. Suspension components
3. Drivetrain upgrades
4. Electronics and control systems
5. Fine-tuning and setup optimization

Remember that driver skill and setup knowledge are just as important as the hardware upgrades. Practice regularly and continue learning about vehicle dynamics to maximize your investment in upgrades.
